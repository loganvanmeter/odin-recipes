Turkish Delight

Description:

A classic confection, Turkish Delight might seem like you need the powers of a witch to pull off, but with this easy recipe, your guests will be transcended to a magical place, no wardrobe needed! Best served with a digestif by a roaring fire, this sweet treat spells winter wonderland and will make anyone feel like royalty. Just watch out for guests warring over who gets the last morsel.

Makes: 100 morsels

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups granulated sugar
  • 41/4 cups water
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice
  • 11/4 cups cornstarch
  • 1 tsp cream of tartar
  • 2 tbsp rosewater
  • 11/2 tsp raspberry extract
  • 11/2 tsp orange extract
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios
  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 4 to 6 drops red food coloring

Directions:

  1. Set two large 4 to 6qt stock pots on the stovetop. Attach a candy thermometer onto on pot. Then set out a 9 x 9in baking dish. Line it neatly with foil, then spray it generously with nonstick cooking spray. Set aside.
  2. Pour 4 cups of granulated sugar and 11/2 cups of water in the pot with the candy thermometer. Set on high head and bring to a boil. Continue to boil until the simple syrup reaches 240°F.
  3. Meanwhile, pour remaining water into the second pot. Whisk in lemon juice, cornstarch, and cream of tartar until there are no clumps. Turn the heat on high and continure whisking until the mixture forms a thick paste that resembles petroleum jelly, again making sure there are no clumps.
  4. Once your simple syrup reaches temp, slowly and carefully pour the syrup into the cornstarch paste, bit by bit.
  5. Turn heat to medium and simmer for approximately 45min, stirring occasionally, until the mixture becomes a thick golden jelly.
  6. Remove from heat, stir in rosewater, raspberry and orange extracts, and salt. Add food coloring, then pour half of the mixture into the prepared baking dish.
  7. Quickly sprinkle with the chopped pistachios, the top with the remaining candy mixture before it becomes too thick to pour.
  8. Chill dish in the refrigerator for 30-60min until firm.
  9. Turn the Turkish Delight out of the dish, and peel the foil off. Use a sharp knife to cut the candy into 100 tiny squares (10 x 10 grid with each square slightly under 1in in size)
  10. Add powdered sugar to an empty baking dish. Shake cut candies to the powdered sugar until they are coated on all sides.
  11. Store in an airtight container at room temperature until ready to serve or up to 4 weeks.
